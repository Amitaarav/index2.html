Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • Nestle Milkmaid : 1/2 tin
  • Flour : 2 cups(200 gms)
  • Grated Paneer : 600 gms
  • Sooji/Rava : 200gms
  • Baking Powder : 1.5 tsp
  • Baking Soda : 1.5 tsp
  • Oil : For Frying
  • Water : 2 ltr
  • Sugar : 1 kg
  • Coarsely Crushed Cardamom : 6-8 nos

How to make Gulab Jamun

  1. Step 1: Boil sugar and water to prepare syrup remove from fire. Add cardamom and keep aside to cool.
  2. Step 2: In a mixing bowl put the flour, paneer, sooji, Nestlé MILKMAID, baking powder and baking soda. Mix gently to make a soft dough. Do not knead too much.
  3. Step 3: Divide the mixture into 30-35 portions and gently roll into round gulab jamuns. Fry in the oil at very low flame till golden brown in color.
  4. Step 4: Keep putting the fried gulab jamuns in the prepared sugar syrup. Once all the gulab jamuns are in the sugar syrup bring it to a boil and remove from flame.
  5. Step 5: Serve warm.

Tips to Gulab Jamun

  • Fry jamuns on low to medium flame to ensure even cooking without browning the outside before the inside cooks.
  • Create small dough balls, as they expand during frying and soaking in syrup.
  • Allow fried jamuns to cool briefly before adding them to warm, not hot, sugar syrup to prevent them from shrinking.
  • Divide the dough into equal parts and gently shape smooth, crack-free balls without kneading or applying excessive pressure.

Kalakand

Ingredients

  • Nestle Milkmaid : 1 tin
  • Nestle Every Dairy Whitener : 2 heaped tbsp
  • Paneer : 500 gms
  • Powderd Elaichi : 3-4
  • Silver leaf : 1

How to make Kalakand

  • Step 1: Mash paneer coarsely and mix in Everyday Dairy Whitener and Nestlé MILKMAID.
  • Step 2: Heat the mixture in a thick bottom pan. Cook on medium heat with constant stirring till the mixture becomes thick and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
  • Step 3: Remove from the fire and spread onto a greased plate. Sprinkle elaichi powder.
  • Step 4: Top with silver leaf.
  • Step 5: Cool and cut into squares.

Tips on Kalakand

  • Opt for fresh, high-quality paneer (cottage cheese) to achieve the most exquisite texture and flavour of Kalakand barfi.
  • When cooking the mixture of paneer and condensed milk, maintain a low to medium heat to prevent it from scorching or sticking to the pan.
  • Exercise caution not to overcook the Kalakand mixture, as excessive cooking may result in the formation of hard, dry cubes of Kalakand once it sets.
  • The desired consistency of the mixture is moist yet thick, not runny. It is at this point that you should remove it from the heat.
  • Allow the Kalakand to set naturally at room temperature or in the refrigerator for a few hours before proceeding to cut it into your desired pieces.
